Objection Handling

Common pushback and how to respond

Shift the conversation from rate to net effective rate (NER). Total Fees ÷ Total Volume = NER. Under 2% is competitive. Ask for their last three statements and run the math together: a bundled statement hides costs that IC+ exposes.

Key move

Request the last three statements and calculate NER together

Validate the concern, then reframe: dual pricing isn't adding a fee, it's displaying two prices. Cash customers get a discount. Most merchants find customers adapt quickly, especially in retail and QSR where posted pricing is already the norm.

Key move

Reframe as two posted prices, not an added fee

That's great. Ask to see their last statement together. Most merchants on bundled pricing don't know their actual NER. A side-by-side comparison is usually the conversation that moves things forward.

Key move

Offer a side-by-side statement comparison

Acknowledge the timing and stay in front of them. Check in at the 90-day mark: that's when early frustrations surface and switching decisions restart. Keep the relationship warm and be the first call when they're ready to move.

Key move

Set a 90-day follow-up reminder

Respect the relationship, don't try to displace it. Ask what the arrangement looks like: are they getting statements? Do they know their NER? Position yourself as a second set of eyes, not a competitor. If the friend is good, they'll say so. If they're not, you've planted a seed.

Key move

Position as a second set of eyes, not a competitor
