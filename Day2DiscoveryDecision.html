Before You Ever Mention Propelr
Prepping for the Discovery Appointment

30 minutes or less. Leave with their last 2 to 3 months of processing statements.

1
Learn their process. How do they handle payments today? Where does it slow down or break? You're building a map, not making a pitch.
2
Identify their frustrations. What's not working? What do they keep tolerating? That gap is your opening.
3
Clarify the decision timeline. When do they make decisions like this? Who else needs to be involved? Knowing the timeline keeps you from chasing a deal that isn't moving.
4
Lock in the next step. Before you leave, lock in the Decision Appointment. You came to learn, now set the meeting where decisions get made.

Running the Meeting
The Decision Appointment

Come back with a solution built around what Discovery taught you. This is the meeting where you ask for the business.

1
Build Rapport. Starts at first impression and never stops. Get curious about them personally, people open up when they feel seen, not sold to.
2
Set the Agenda. Tell them what you'd like to cover and why. Be upfront about the time. Structure signals you respect their day.
3
Fact-Finding. Aim for 70% of your time here. Uncover what they value, where they're frustrated, and what Propelr can genuinely solve.
4
Qualify Next Steps. Find their timeline. Identify what's in the way. Use the "If I can, will you" framework to test readiness before you come back with a solution.
