Merchant Pays

Interchange Plus (IC+)

Every transaction = interchange rate (set by Visa/MC) + your processor's fixed markup. Both appear separately on the statement. The most transparent model and the easiest to justify with a side-by-side comparison.

How to read a statement

Push for itemized IC+ statements. Bundled statements hide costs. Itemized lets you spot card mix issues and B2B optimization opportunities.

Card mix matters

Rewards, corporate, and premium cards carry higher interchange — set by the card networks, not the processor. This is your opening to talk about cost offsets.

Best for: Merchants wanting full visibility, professional services, high-volume retail, B2B.

Merchant Offsets

Dual Pricing

Merchant marks up listed prices to the "card price." Cash customers are discounted back down — up to 4%. The card price IS the advertised price. No fee added at point of sale.

What customers see

Card price and cash price displayed side-by-side at checkout. No surprise fee — that's the key distinction from surcharging.

Compatible platforms

Valor PayTech (VP100, VP550, VP800) and SwipeSimple (A920 Pro, A80). Specify cash discount % in your deployment request notes.

Not supported on Square.

Best for: Retail, QSR, food trucks, any merchant with fixed posted pricing.

Customer Pays

Surcharge Program

A percentage fee (1–3%) added at checkout for credit card transactions only. BIN lookup on compliant devices auto-recognizes and excludes debit automatically.

Compliance requirements

  • Posted signage required before the sale
  • Must apply at all locations — no cherry-picking
  • Not available in CT or MA

Card network rules

Applied to Visa, MC, Discover, and AMEX OptBlue credit only. Surcharge never applies to debit or prepaid — BIN lookup handles this automatically.

Best for: Credit-heavy card mix, merchants wanting full fee elimination.
