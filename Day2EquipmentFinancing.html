Funding the Deployment
Equipment Financing

Propelr offers seven ways to fund equipment. The preferred path is the Merchant Upfront Purchase, simplest and fastest to close. The other six exist to overcome budget objections, at a cost, and Pilot-facing options need finance approval.

Sales Note
  • Alternatives to the upfront purchase may carry higher long-term costs or increased liability exposure.
  • Any exception to financing policy requires written approval from our COO, Mike Morsell.
  • Operations will not order equipment until all required documentation is received.
1
Merchant Upfront Purchase. Preferred. Merchant pays sale price upfront.
2
Merchant Installment (3 or 6 mo). Merchant pays monthly, titles at payoff.
3
Merchant Lease. Third-party lessor (TCG), profit split with Pilot.
4
Merchant Rental. Propelr owns equipment, Pilot Surety required.
5
Pilot Residual Offset. Pilot recovers cost from their own residuals.
6
Margin Offset Sharing. Cost split and recovered from MID margin.
7
100% Propelr-Funded. Propelr pays, Pilot split permanently reduced 25%.
