Boarding an Account

Move your test lead from a lead to a live merchant, tab by tab.

1
You should have created a test lead on Day 1 labeled "[Your Name] - Test Account #1." Let's learn how to move it forward and take it from a lead to a live merchant.
2
When you created your test lead, you randomly chose the Platform and Pricing Type. Verify this is correct before moving forward. Once verified, select Save Lead.
3
In the Lead Information tab, fill out all of the fields. If the merchant doesn't have a web address, leave that field blank, entering N/A or an invalid site will generate an error.

  • If the Lead Information tab isn't filled out correctly, the deal will be pended.
4
Work from left to right through all of the tabs, saving often, especially before moving to a new tab.
5
Make sure you've accurately completed all fields, verified your pricing and scheduled rates, and entered parameters for the merchant's software and hardware.
6
Look for Online Agreement Forms within the MPA.
7
Select Form Share. If working with Woodforest, select WFNB MPA. If working with Fiserv, select Fiserv North Flat.
8
Select Generate & Send Email.
9
Always choose Webform or Online Form.
10
A preview window will pop up. Verify the email address. Do not click the link within the email yourself, doing so assigns the session to your IP and the merchant won't be able to access it.
11
Once the merchant has accessed the link, they need to complete the application in the same session, or you'll need to archive the MPA and re-generate it.
Keep Your Pipeline Moving
Boarding Quality: Common Errors to Avoid

Small data entry mistakes are the biggest source of pend delays. Open the category that applies to what you're submitting.

  • Platform changes must happen before the MPA is sent or signed. Changes after the fact affect required fields and can alter the deal entirely, same rule applies to retail-to-wholesale flips
  • Select the correct processing network. Fiserv applications need a Fiserv network selected. The most common error is submitting with TSYS selected instead, double-check any time a platform change is involved
  • Fiserv Wholesale deals need the correct Sales Code from CoPilot for that deal type. If you haven't been issued one, reach out to partnerdev@propelrpay.com
  • IC+ pricing is entered as a percentage, not basis points. Remember, bps are 1/100th of a percent, double-check the format before submitting
  • Internet sales percentage should be left blank when the merchant uses a Virtual Terminal or a digital invoicing process. Adding one anyway is a data entry error we're seeing more often
  • Surcharge rate cap: card brand rules cap surcharge at 4% (not to exceed the cost of processing), but Fiserv has its own cap of 3%. The surcharge addendum is now built directly into the Flat Rate/Surcharge application in Stratus
  • Owner 1 details and the Control Prong must both be filled out completely and must match each other. Partial owner info that conflicts with the Control Prong is a recurring issue, double-check before submission
  • Federal credit unions won't authenticate through GIACT. Get a voided check and/or bank letter, best practice is to ask for this documentation upfront regardless of bank type
  • Website field should only contain the merchant's real URL. Entering a placeholder like "nowebsite.com" or "NA" disrupts underwriting automations, if the merchant has no website, handle it as its own case rather than faking one
  • Email addresses need to be valid and properly formatted before submission
Not for Sales Partners

The duplicate feature is limited to Operations teams. If it's ever used, every field should reflect only the new lead, so:

  • Switch the Category to Lead Development
  • Deselect everything except "Link to Original," "Copy Custom Properties," and "Assign to Me"
  • Clear out fields that should be blank for the new lead, such as the MID

Funding the Deployment
Equipment Financing

Propelr offers seven ways to fund equipment. The preferred path is the Merchant Upfront Purchase, simplest and fastest to close. The other six exist to overcome budget objections, at a cost, and Pilot-facing options need finance approval.

Sales Note
  • Alternatives to the upfront purchase may carry higher long-term costs or increased liability exposure.
  • Any exception to financing policy requires written approval from our COO, Mike Morsell.
  • Operations will not order equipment until all required documentation is received.
1
Merchant Upfront Purchase. Preferred. Merchant pays sale price upfront.
2
Merchant Installment (3 or 6 mo). Merchant pays monthly, titles at payoff.
3
Merchant Lease. Third-party lessor (TCG), profit split with Pilot.
4
Merchant Rental. Propelr owns equipment, Pilot Surety required.
5
Pilot Residual Offset. Pilot recovers cost from their own residuals.
6
Margin Offset Sharing. Cost split and recovered from MID margin.
7
100% Propelr-Funded. Propelr pays, Pilot split permanently reduced 25%.
